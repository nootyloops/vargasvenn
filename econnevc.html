New England Colonies

★ More focused on God and community rather then money

★ Did not use slaves, many people were Abolitionists as well

★ Multi-crop economy, farmers did not plant for the intention of mass profit, mostly for food and sustainment

★ Much less class difference, no huge difference due to societal attitudes

★ Sought to improve artisan industry, more "home-based" then pure profit

★ Most industries were "home-based" industries, and farms were mostly for family purposes

★ Attitude torwards land and economy; would rather have it grow not as fast, but have all families roughly close to each other in terms of wealth

Cheasepeake Colonies

★ Central focus was on money -nothing less

★ Utilized slaves to farm on plantations, vital to their economy

★ Mostly a single crop economy, with Tobacco being utilized by John Rolfe, and cotton as a another popular cash crop

★ Huge class differences, due to slaves and identured workers unable to set down roots due to harsh lifestyle -whilst plantation owners kept on getting richer

★ Came wanting as much land as possible, seeking gold and silver as well

★ As Cheasepeake marshy land was poor for growing most crops, there was a heavy dependence on the revenue generated by cash crops

★ "Get Rich Quick" attitude, little regard to growing and obtaining basic supplies -in favor of searching for minerals and cash crops
