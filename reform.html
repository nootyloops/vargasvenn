Founded: 1774

Origin: New England

Major Cities: Boston, New York City, Baltimore, Washington, Charleston

2nd Awakening Connection: Both bolstered popularity in Prtoestant Christianity, led to increased membership and actiivty, a departure from more common methods of Christianity

Effectiveness: Unitarianism was masssively successful in America, garnering a massive population in a relatively short amount of time; spreading from Massachusetts into multiple states and cities, boasted an impressive membership

American Temperance Society

Founder(s): Clergymen

Founded: 1826

Origin: Boston

Major Cities: Boston

2nd Awakening Connection: The American Temperance Society yet another reform movement promoting their ideologies, as they fought for widespread acceptance

Effectiveness: The movement successfully altered drinking habits of middle-class American Males. Campaign was extremely effective as by 1834 they were five thousand local branches with more than a million members , especially focused around heavy liquors.

Marriage for Love

Founder(s): Romance Novels

Founded: All across the United States

Origin: All across the United States

Major Cities: White Middle Class

2nd Awakening Connection: Inspired new conceptions of its role in American society, like this concept. Evangelical cultures influenced the family as an institution.

Effectiveness: It became easier for sons to marry while their father weres still alive and for younger daughters to wed before their oldest sisters— Reflecting weakening position of power of parents regarding the marriage of their children.

Cult of Domesticity (“Cult of True Womanhood”)

Founder(s): White men

Founded: Mid-nineteenth century

Origin: All across the untied states

Major Cities: Suburban Women

2nd Awakening Connection: This was an evangelical movement which presented the man object of worship a Christ with stereotypical feminine characteristic— Quietly expressing feminine influence

Effectiveness: By evangelical standards, women could be viewed as superior to men, since women were in a good position to cultivate “feminine” virtues of love and self-sacrifice and thus act as official guardians of religious and moral values. Women of mid-nineteenth century did not feel oppressed by the new arrangement as the new norm of confinement to the home did not necessarily imply that women were inferior

Major Cities: New England

2nd Awakening Connection:The revivals gave women a role in converting men and presented the main object of worship a Christ with feminine characteristics Membership in evangelical church-based associations inspired and prepared women for new roles as civilizers of men and guardians of domestic culture and morality

Education Reform (Public School Movement)

Founder(s): Horace Mann

Founded: 1820-1850

Origin: Founded in Massachusetts

Major Cities: New England cities

2nd Awakening Connection: A movement that pushed for replacing the family role of teaching values to children McGuffey’s Eclectic Readers taught children moral indoctrination

Effectiveness: Effective because Massachusetts established board of education. Not so effective because it alienated working class students. Attendance laws took wage earners from poorer families

Prison Reform

Founder(s): Dorothea Dix

Founded: 1820-1830

Origin: New York

Major Cities: New York, Philadelphia

2nd Awakening Connection: Custodians of prisons were to provide moral advice and training. Solitary confinement for inmates to reflect on sins without corrupting others

Effectiveness: Not effective because prisons, asylums, and poorhouses didn’t achieve their aims. There wasn’t enough public support to meet needs of inmate population and personnel lacked training. Failed to reform criminals

Asylum Reform

Founder(s): Dorothea Dix

Founded: 1840

Origin: New England

Major Cities: Boston, Pennslyvania, New York

2nd Awakening Connection: Emphasize treatment and rehabilitation; similiar to rights movement, much more tender take; not as harsh

Effectiveness: Very efffective; by the beginning of the Civil War most states had established public mental institutions, and the practice of keeping the mentally ill in jails and almshouses was in decline

White Abolition Movement

Founder(s): William Lloyd Garrison

Founded: 1820

Origin: Northern States

Major Cities: Northern Cities

2nd Awakening Connection: Preachers like Lyman Beecher, Nathaniel Taylor, and Charles G. Finney in what came to be called the Second Great Awakening led massive religious revivals in the 1820s; preaching ideals to the later emergence of abolitionism

Effectiveness: Massively effective, eventually the majority of the North was abolitionist; segwaying into the Civil War

Shakers

Founder(s): Ann Lee

Founded: 1780

Origin: New York

Major Cities: New York City

2nd Awakening Connection:Espousing egalitarian ideals, women took on spiritual leadership roles alongside men; in line with ideals of the Second Great Awakening and Evangelical Christianity

Effectiveness:Peaked in the mid-19th century, there were 4,000–6,000 Shaker believers living in 18 major communities and numerous smaller, often short-lived, communities; eventually thinned out

Transcendentalism (Brook Farm)

Founder(s): George Ripley

Founded: 1841

Origin: Boston

Major Cities: Boston

2nd Awakening Connection: Based on balancing labor and leisure while working together for the better of the greater community; each member could choose to do whatever work they found most appealing and all were paid equally, including women

Effectiveness: Popular and effective, people swarmed to the new labour system and relatively egalitarian lifestyle; major Utopian Society
