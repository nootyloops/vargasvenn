Question 1
Time Period: 1, 1491-1607
Key Concept: 1.1 A
Themes: Culture and Society
Skills: Contextualization
Question: Evaluate the cultural and economical impact maize had on early Native American societies.
Thesis Statement: Maize was held in cultural regard as a grain from the gods, alongside its role as key crop for modern day corn, spreading from South America to North America. This was crucial, as in societies both past and future relied heavily on cheap carbs and energy to fuel nations.
Connections:
☆ Cash crops, although not for food, people also heavily depended on crops
☆ Cultural signifigance, food viewed as sacred/from the Gods (ex: Ambrosia; Greece)
☆ Economically, used for trade, although more valuable trading foodstuffs were used too
☆ Tribute system, maize was a part, similiar to European tribute systems
Contextuality:
☆ Most nations in South America relied on Maize heavily for food
☆ Crucial, provided most raw and cheap energy
☆ Predecessor to modern corn
Complexity: While maize was popular, similiar crops such as the 3 sisters or cacao were just as important and valued in Native American societies.

Key Concept: ​1.2 (B)
Themes:​ Work and Exchange of Technology, Economy
Skills:​ Causation
Question:​ Analyze the effects that New World food crops had on European populations and economic systems.
Thesis:​ Because of the high caloric content of New World food crops like the potato, European populations could expand dramatically with the addition of those new crops. New World crops provided variety in the European diet, which provided for a better fed European population. Due to a stronger farmer class from the control of more productive crops, farmers could demand less hierarchical powers, leading to a shift from feudal economic structures to capitalism.
Connections to other time periods: Massive demand for Southern cotton and tobacco following the introduction of cotton and tobacco to the 13 colonies
Contextuality: This dependence on cheap grains has been reflected in Asian culturesv(rice), maize in latin america and so forth, with cheap energy always in demand
Complexity: Such dependence on easy energy could backfire if a main crop was unable to produce, ex: Irish Potato Famine

Question 7
Time Period: ​1607-1754
Key Concept: ​2.1.1 (C)
Themes:​ Migration, Society and Culture
Skills:​ Causation
Question:​ Analyze the reasons for the movement of British immigrants to the American colonies.
Thesis Statement:​ British immigrants came in large numbers to the American colonies due to the comparatively great opportunities for middle class men in the colonies than in Britain, due partially to the overcrowding of British cities and the cheap land available in the American colonies. Some were motivated by the freedom of religion available in the colonies, particularly persecuted minorities such as the Puritans in Massachusetts Bay.
Connections:
☆ Growing immigration to the United States after independence
☆ Immigration from Asia to the United States
Contextualization:
☆ Barbadian connection
☆ Urbanization in Britain
☆ Religious toleration in the American colonies.
Complexity:
☆ Reasons for moving from Britain were similar to moving from Barbados to Georgia - much cheaper land, less crowded
☆ Land was cheap because Britain wanted to encourage settlement in the colonies in order to project military power into North America, as per mercantilist ideals.

Thesis Statement:​ Due to the bad experience with a monarch, and the fact that monarchs were abhorred by republican values, the colonists avoided placing power into the hands of an executive official when possible, instead choosing to give the power to legislatures.
Many states went so far as to state that they were sovereign, not members of a single federation, which changed after the ratification of the Constitution.
Connections:
☆ Nullification Crisis
☆ Lack of government power under articles of Confederation
Contextualization:
☆ Abolition of governor position in Pennsylvania and Georgia
☆ Commonwealthmen tradition would dictate that not much power go into central hands
☆ Legislatures had power of the purse to rein in executive power.
Complexity:
☆ Government also had to balance control with a judicial branch after the Judiciary Act of 1789
☆ The sovereignty of states only became a non-issue after the War of Northern Aggression.
